Air pollution costs lives

Given current air pollution levels worldwide, human lives are shorter due to premature dealth from air pollution-related illnesses. Studies show that the impact of air pollution is higher than the impact of any other cause of death, including communicable diseases like tuberculosis and HIV/AIDS, illnesses caused by smoking and poor water sanitation, and death from conflicts and war.
